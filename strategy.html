The Strategy Playbook

Which content to serve, to whom, when — and the psychological mechanisms behind each decision. This is the full Learnix recommendation engine spec, grounded in Prochaska TTM, Gollwitzer Implementation Intentions, and Berridge's wanting-vs-liking framework.

⚠ Anti-Patterns That Kill Behavior Change

These 5 mechanisms are why motivational content consumption substitutes for rather than drives change. Learnix must be designed to short-circuit all of them.

Symbolic Self-Completion
Wicklund & Gollwitzer, 1982
Saving motivational content feels like progress. It is not.
⚡ When users save a video about discipline, their brain logs 'identity acquired'. The urgency to actually be disciplined drops. This is why heavy savers rarely act.
Do not let saves substitute for tasks. Every save prompts: 'What's one thing you'll do today based on this?' Force the bridge.
Moral Licensing
Khan & Dhar, 2006
Engaging with virtue content creates a 'virtue credential' that licenses vice.
⚡ A user who watches a 10-minute Goggins video feels they've done their 'discipline work' for the day. They're less likely to work out afterwards, not more.
Never serve discipline/shame content right before a task reminder. Serve it after task completion as celebration fuel.
Berridge Wanting vs Liking
Kent Berridge, 1998
Dopamine drives wanting (seeking), not liking (pleasure). Save = the dopamine hit.
⚡ The act of saving is the reward. Once saved, dopamine drops. There is no further pull toward action from the content itself.
Make the TASK the dopamine trigger, not the content. Content should be an after-action reward, not a pre-action ritual.
Implementation Intentions
Gollwitzer, 1999
IF-THEN plans double execution rate (meta-analysis d=0.65).
⚡ Most motivation content says WHAT to do, not WHEN + WHERE + HOW EXACTLY. Specificity is the active ingredient.
Every IF-THEN content piece should prompt the user to write their own version: 'When X happens, I will do Y.' Store this in their Learnix profile.
Self-Discrepancy Theory
Higgins, 1987
Gap between actual-self and ideal-self creates emotional tension that drives change.
⚡ Too much gap = hopelessness. Too little gap = complacency. The sweet spot is 'I can almost reach it.'
Calibrate content difficulty to the user's current CRS. Show them someone 1-2 steps ahead, not 10 steps ahead.

TECHNICAL TUTORIAL IF-THEN PLANS PROCESS / DISCIPLINE
Tutorials and plans signal readiness they don't have. Serving plans to pre-cons accelerates Symbolic Self-Completion: they feel like they've 'started' by watching a plan video.
Core Mechanism
Pattern Interruption + Loss Aversion
Kahneman: losses hurt 2× more than equal gains feel good. Pre-cons respond to vivid loss framing, not gain framing. Interrupt the existing narrative — don't add to it.

Upgrade Signal

SHAME / FEAR HOOK MINDSET / PHILOSOPHY
Shame is corrosive when someone is already trying — it triggers discouragement not urgency. Philosophy is fine but it's a DISTRACTION from doing. Action users need doing content, not thinking content.
Core Mechanism
Competence Building + Vicarious Achievement
Bandura's Self-Efficacy: vicarious mastery (watching others do it) is the second strongest predictor of persistence, after direct mastery. Also: Huberman's work on dopamine timing — rewarding the EFFORT (not the outcome) keeps the motivation circuit healthy.
Learnix Trigger
CRS 70–85 OR task completion rate > 60% for 2+ weeks
Upgrade Signal
CRS > 85 OR 6+ week streak OR user completes a milestone goal.
